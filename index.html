How to

Train your llama

Dealing with disobedience? No prob llama! Our experienced team has been training llamas for generations. We've seen and experienced it all! This guide will lay out exactly how to train your llama to be the perfect companion.

What to expect

Llamas are very intelligent animals who can learn many things, and it is easy to train them. Every llama should know some basics:

  • To allow you to halter him quickly and easily
  • To walk along with you when on a leash, keeping the leash loose
  • To jump into a van, pickup, or trailer for transporting
  • To let you handle their body, so you can do grooming or check injuries

Training Facilities

We recommend having a catch pen. LLamas will happily enter and they can be held there safely and securely! If space allows, it would be great to have stables and dedicated training areas. We recommend an area at least 6'x6'. This will provide enough room to catch the llama and manoeuvre them safely. Make sure there aren't any sharp edges that could injure you or your llama.

Equipment

Make sure you have the right equipment! You will need at least a halter that fits correctly and a good lead rope to fix onto the halter. There are other items that are desirable but not essential. These non-essential items include a small bamboo cane to help guide the llama and a small bag to hold some feed for rewarding.

Repetition

No matter what particular training exercise you are performing with your llamas, whether that is haltering, leading, desensitising, brushing, trekking, fitting a pack, etc, the exercise must be repeated over and over again until the llama fully understands and accepts what it is you are trying to get it to do (and that it will not be harmed in the process). Repeat frequently and keep your training sessions short and simple!
